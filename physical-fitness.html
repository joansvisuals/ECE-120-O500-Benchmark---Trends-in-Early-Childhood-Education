Physical Fitness.

not merely an exercise

Another important Trend that has gained momentum in physical fitness is innovative play. This is not merely an exercise; it is making young children aware of the importance of physical health and well-being from an early age, thereby helping them become actively involved in their own well-being by eating right and staying healthy.

It strengthens and enhances flexibility, builds small- and large-motor skills, and, with consistency, supports cognitive development, which is important for learning.

A report by EBSCO (2021) stated that going beyond routine play and incorporating aerobic games and obstacle courses regularly improves young children's long-jumping ability, speed, and endurance, and makes them more agile by strengthening their leg muscles.

Physical fitness is not merely trendy; it has a positive impact on the health and development of young children, extending into Adulthood. Age-appropriate physical activities are essential for children in early education. It helps in the fight against childhood obesity and juvenile diabetes, and creates healthier adults for the future.
